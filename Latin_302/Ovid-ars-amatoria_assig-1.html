xx

Ars Amatoria



Ovid promises that his "hook-up manual" will provide expert knowledge. It will also make his name famous as the master-craftsman of romantic relationships.







Sī quis in hōc artem populō nōn nōvit amandī, Hoc legat et lectō carmine doctus amet. Arte citae vēlōque ratēs rēmōque moventur, Arte levēs currūs: arte regendus amor. Curribus Automedōn lentīsque erat aptus habēnīs,      5 Tīphys in Haemoniā puppe magister erat: Mē Venus artificem tenerō praefēcit Amōrī; Tīphys et Automedōn dīcar Amōris ego.







Ovid rejects the traditional sources of knowledge for a poet. He has gained his expertise by the scientific method: trial and error.




Nōn ego , Phoebe , datās ā tē mihi mentiar artēs, Nec nōs āëriae vōce monēmur avis, Nec mihi sunt vīsae Clīō Clīūsque sorōrēs Servantī pecudēs vallibus, Ascra, tuīs: Ūsus opus movet hoc: vātī pārēte perītō; Vēra canam: coeptīs , māter Amōris , ades ! Este procul, vittae tenuēs, insigne pudōris, Quaeque tegis mediōs, instita longa, pedēs. Nōs venerem tūtam concessaque furta canēmus, Inque meō nūllum carmine crīmen erit .

Notes

l. 1: quis. After sī, quis has the meaning of aliquis, "anyone."

l. 2: carmen, carminis, neu. A poem (not the rare meaning "a card for wool").

l. 2: doctus is a secondary predication. It does not so much specify the subject as it modifies amet. It can be rendered something like "as a learned man."

l. 3: citae. Perfect Passive Participle of cieō. It means "speedy."

l. 3: vēlum, vēlī, neu. A sail.

l. 4: lĕvis, lĕve. With a short e in the stem, it means "light." With a long e, "smooth."

l. 5: Curribus ... habēnīs. The adjective aptus is often further qualified by a dative: "suitable for x."

l. 5: Automedōn, Automedontis, masc. Automedon. The name of the charioteer of the famous warrior Achilles.

l. 6: Tīphys, Tīphyos, masc. Tiphys. The name of the pilot of the famous ship Argo.

l. 6: Haemonius, -a, -um. Haemonia is an old word for Thessaly, where Argo came from. Translate "Haemonian."

l. 7: praefēcit. This verb means "put x in charge of y." The person put in charge is in the ACC; the matter one is put in chage of is in the DAT.

l. 7: artificem. A secondary predication: "as an expert."
