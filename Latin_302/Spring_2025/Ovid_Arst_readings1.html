Selections from Ovid's Ars Amatoria

1

Sī quis in hōc artem populō nōn nōvit amandī,

Hoc legat et lectō carmine doctus amet.

  • quis: = "anyone"
  • amandī: gerund, sing. neut. gen. = "loving"
  • lectō carmine: best as ABL absolute
  • doctus: a secondary predication; this structure is often best rendered into English with "as"
2

Arte citae vēlōque ratēs rēmōque moventur,

Arte levēs currūs: arte regendus amor.

  • vēlō: vēlum, vēlī, neu. = "sail"
  • -que: conj., X-que = "and X"
  • levēs: the two adjectives levis, -e, and lēvis, -e are easily confused. lev- with a short e in the stem = light; lēv- with a long e = "smooth"
  • currūs: = "chariots"
  • regendus: gerundive, also called future passive participle. Here regendus is short for regendus est. This combiniation is often called the "passive periphrastic construction." It is always debitive and passive = SBJ must be verbed.
3

Curribus Automedōn lentīsque erat aptus habēnīs, 5

Tīphys in Haemoniā puppe magister erat:

  • Automedōn, Automedōn Automedontis, mas. PrN (proper name); the charioteer of Achilles
  • aptus: aptus, apta, aptum = "adept." Often + DAT (= "adept at DAT")
  • Tīphys, sing. mas. nom., PrN; the pilot of the Argo
  • Haemoniā, Haemonius, Haemonia, Haemonium = "Thessalian." Thessaly is a PLN (proper name of a place)
4

Mē Venus artificem tenerō praefēcit Amōrī;

Tīphys et Automedōn dīcar Amōris ego.

  • artificem: = "expert." A secondary predication; translate "a an expert"
  • praefēcit: verb frame = SBJ puts X in charge or Y, where X is ACC and Y is DAT
  • Amōrī: = "love" here personified as the son of Venus = "Cupid"
  • dīcar: verb frame = SBJ is called X, where X is the same case as SBJ
5

Nōn ego , Phoebe , datās ā tē mihi mentiar artēs,

Nec nōs āëriae vōce monēmur avis, 10

Nec mihi sunt vīsae Clīō Clīūsque sorōrēs

Servantī pecudēs vallibus, Ascra, tuīs:

  • Phoebe: Phoebus, Phoebī, mas. A PrN referring to Apollo
  • datās: = datās esse = PERF PASS INFIN
  • ā: prep + ABL(X) = "through the agency of/by X"
  • mentiar: verb frame = SBJ falsely states X, where X is an indirect statement clause
  • avis: perhaps a recherché reference to prophecy. Zeus was said to have sent a dove to Dodona in Nothwest Greece. The dove landed there in a tree and spoke in a human voice, commanding that an shrine and oracle to Zeus be built in that location.
  • mihi: = "by me"
  • Clīō: Clīō, Clīūs (a Greek form of the gen.) sing. fem. nom; PrN of one of the goddesses know as the Muses. Clio is often considered the goddess of the writing of history
  • Ascra: Ascra, Ascrae, fem. PLN of a small town in Boeotia, a region of Greece. Here Hesiod, the inventor of didactic poetry, was watching over his sheep when the Muses appeared to him and gave him the gift of poetry.
