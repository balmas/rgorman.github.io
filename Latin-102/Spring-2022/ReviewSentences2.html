Practice Sentences Latin 102

1
hortātur, ut rem pūblicam suscipiant.
hortātur is deponent. What kind of clause is suscipiant? How do you know?
2
ab arātrō abdūxērunt Cincinnātum, ut dictātor esset.
Cincinnātus is the name of a person; it is ACC here.
3
Caesar, ut ad sē redūcerentur, imperāvit.
Remember that sē is a reflexive pronoun. What does that mean?
4
tanta vīs probitātis est, ut eam in hoste dīligāmus.
Note that est indicates as state, not an action that can be controlled. This fact helps us identify the kind of clause of dīligāmus.
5
reliquōs, ut idem facerent, hortātus est.
hortātus est count as a single verb; it is deponent.
6
ut sint auxiliō suīs, subsistunt.
Even though auxiliō is DAT, it is functions as the predicate nominal; we will discuss this common phenomenon.
7
nē mīlitēs oppidum inrumperent, obstruit portās.
The presence of nē tells us that the inrumperent clause has directive illocutionary force. This fact helps us decide what kind of clause to call it.
8
id ut faciās, etiam atque etiam tē hortor.
etiam atque etiam can be translated "over and over."
9
magnō opere tē hortor, ut tē ad nōs cōnferās.
hortor is deponent.
10
cōpiās trānsdūxit eō cōnsiliō, ut castellum expugnāret.
eō cōnsiliō can be considered an ABL of cause; the relevant meaning is probably "intention"; see dictionary section II.A. A good translation is "with this intention."
11
petunt ā Vercingetorige Aeduī, ut ad sē veniat.
Aeduī is NOM PLUR.
12
multīs gravibusque vulneribus cōnfectus est, ut iam sē sustinēre nōn posset.
cōnfectus est means "was weakened" (see definition II.A.). The cause of the weakening is regularly given by an ABL; if the ABL is required by this meaning of the verb, vulneribus is more correctly OBJ than ADV.
13
tantā vī in Pompeī equitēs impetum fēcērunt ut eōrum nēmō cōnsisteret.
cōnsisteret probably here means "stand one's ground" as in resist an attack (see defintion II.B.2).
14
mē rogās et hortāris, ut apud tē in Ēpīrō sim.
Prepositional phrases such as apud tē can function as Predicate Nominals. Consider this English example: "He is tired and in a bad mood."
15
huic mandat, ut ad sē quam prīmum revertātur.
revertātur is deponent. mandat here has the meaning "order" and the verb frame SBJ orders DAT that (UT + SUBJ), where the finite complement clause is the thing/action that is ordered (see dictionary sect. I.b).
16
tam longē aberam ut nōn vidērem.
aberam here means "be at a distance." We would normally expect vidērem to have an OBJ. We assume the original context made that element clear.
17
scālās parārī iubet, nē quam facultātem dīmittat.
