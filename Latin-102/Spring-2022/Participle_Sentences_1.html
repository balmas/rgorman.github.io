Latin 102
Participial Clauses 1

1
hoc spērāns legiōnēs trīs ex castrīs ēdūcit.
What is the function of spērāns (what kind of part. clause)?
2
Caelius profectus ad Caesarem pervēnit Thūriōs.
Caelius is the name of a certain Roman man. Thūriī is the name of a certain town in Italy.
3
heri dederam ad tē litterās exiēns ē Puteolānō.
Puteolānum is the name of a certain country estate.
4
prīdiē Īdūs Fundīs accēpī tuās litterās cēnāns.
Fundī is the name of a certain town in Italy.
5
hās tibi litterās exiēns in Tusculānum scrīpsī.
Tusculānum is the name of a certain country estate.
6
inde per Ligurēs Appennīnum trānsiit petēns Etrūriam.
Ligurēs is the name of a certain Italian people. Appennīnus is the name of an Italian mountain range.
7
Pompeius idōneum locum nactus ibi cōpiās collocāvit.
Pompeius is the name of a famous Roman general.
8
itinera fēcit multō maiōra fugiēns quam ego sequēns.
quam is a subordinating conjunction introducing a (non-equivalent) comparison clause.
9
tamen cōnscendēns iam nāvem Ephesō Lāodicēam revertit.
Ephesus is the name of a town in Asia Minor. Likewise for Lāodicēa.
10
reliquōs omnīs equitātū cōnsecūtī nostrī interfēcērunt.
cōnsecūtī is deponent.
11
quīntō Kalendās mane accēpī ā Dēmeā litterās prīdiē datās.
Dēmea is the name of a certain man.
12
ēgressus inde, diēs noctēsque iter faciēns Taurum trānsiit.
Taurus is the name of a mountain range in Anatolia.
13
prīnceps decima legiō per tribūnōs mīlitum eī grātiās ēgit.
prīnceps functions as a secondary predication.
14
eōdem diē lēgātī ab hostibus missī ad Caesarem dē pāce vēnērunt.
ab hostibus expresses the agent of the part. missī.
15
cōnfēcit prior iter Caesar atque contrā hostem aciem īnstruit.
prior functions as a secondary predication.
16
tibi autem crēbrō ad mē scrībentī dē eius officiō nihil rescrīpsī.
