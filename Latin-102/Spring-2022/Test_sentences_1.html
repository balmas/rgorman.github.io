Translation Sentences 10

volō, ut mihi respondeās
1
14 vocab too
volō, ut mihi respondeās tū.
respondeās: 2nd sing pres subj act, verb of FCC. volō: 1st sing pres indic act.
2
ab eō, nē id faceret, petēbant.
faceret 3rd sing imperfect subj act, FCC; for the ab eō see verb frame info for petēbant.
3
ā tē petō, ut hanc rem suscipiās.
suscipiās: 2nd sing present subj act, FCC. For ā tē see the verb frame info for petō.
4
tamen, id nē saepius faciās, rogō.
faciās: 2nd sing present subj act.
5
rescrībās nōbīs ad omnia rogāmus.
rescrībās: 2nd sing present subj act; FCC. Note that the subordinator is here

∅ (i.e., there is no expressed subordinator)

6
dant negōtium, ut eum interficiant.
interficiant: 3rd plur present subj act, FCC. dant negōtium ≅ "give an effort."
7
tū mē hortāris, ut animō sim magnō.
sim: 1st sing pres subj act, FCC. hortāris: 2nd sing pres ind passive; this verb is Deponent. animō magnō is ABL meaning "with a great mind" = "with courage."
8
in prīmīs ā tē petō, ut tē ibi videam.
videam: 1st sing present subj act, FCC. For ā tē see verb frame info for petō. in prīmīs is an idiom ≅ "most of all."
9
hortātur, ut sē et Appium sequantur.
sequantur: 3rd plur present subj passive; this verb is Deponent, FCC. hortātur is Deponent.
10
hīs placuit, ut tū in Cūmānum venīrēs.
placuit: "it was pleasing." The content of the "it" is given by the FCC clause. venīrēs: 2nd sing present subj act, FCC. hīs is PLUR/DAT; see verb frame of placet.
11
hortātur, ut rem pūblicam suscipiant.
suscipiant: 3rd plural present subj act, FCC. hortātur is Deponent.
12
tum tibi placuit, ut mitterem ad Sīlium.
placuit: "it was pleasing." The content of the "it" is given by the FCC clause. mitterem: 1st sing imperfect subj act, FCC.
13
Caesar, ut ad sē redūcerentur, imperāvit.
redūcerentur: 3rd plur imperfect subj act, FCC.
14
reliquōs, ut idem facerent, hortātus est.
facerent: 3rd plur imperfect subj act, FCC. hortātus est together make the 3rd sing perfect indicative passive; this verb is Deponent.
15
id ut faciās, etiam atque etiam tē hortor.
faciās: 2nd sing pres subj act, FCC; hortor: 1st sing pres ind; it is Deponent. etiam atque etiam may be translated idiomatically "again and again."
16
magnō opere tē hortor, ut tē ad nōs cōnferās.
cōnferās: 2nd sing present subj act, FCC. tē cōnferās ("bring yourself" = "come"). hortor: see # 15. magnō opere = "with great force."
17
mē rogās et hortāris, ut apud tē in Ēpīrō sim.
sim: 1st sing present subj act of sum, FCC. rogās, hortāris: 2nd sing pres ind; hortāris is Deponent.
18
huic mandat, ut ad sē quam prīmum revertātur.
revertātur: 3rd sing pres subj deponent, FCC. For DAT with mandat see verb frame in vocab. Idiomatically, quam prīmum = "as soon as possible."
19
rogat magnō opere, ut sim Kalendīs in senātū.
sim: 1st sing pres subj act of sum, FCC. For magnō opere see #16. Kalendīs is ABL of point in time.
20
senātus ita dēcrēvit, ut cum legiōnibus īret.
īret: 3rd sing imperfect subj act of eō ("go"), FCC.
21
petunt ā Vercingetorige Aeduī, ut ad sē veniat.
veniat: 3rd sing present subj active, FCC. For petunt with ab + ABL, see verb frame in vocab.
22
intereā nē propius castra movēret, petiērunt.
movēret: 3rd sing imperfect subj act, FCC. petiērunt: 3rd plur perfect indicative active.
23
eō partem nāvium longārum convenīre imperat.
convenīre: Pres Infinitive Active, ICC. eō = ADV ("to that place").
24
ab eō petīvī, ut quam prīmum tē nōbīs redderet.
redderet" 3rd sing imperfect subj act, FCC. petīvī: 1st sing perfect indicative act; see verb frame in vocab for more info. For quam prīmum see #18.
25
nōn hortāris, ut celerius Brundisium veniāmus.
veniāmus: 1st plur present subj act, FCC. Brundisium: ACC proper name of city. hortāris: 2nd sing present indic deponent.
26
magnopere tē hortor, ut hōs etiam librōs lēgās.
lēgās: 2nd sing present subj active, FCC. hortor: 1st sing present subj act.
27
ad frātrem mīsī, ut crēbrō tabellāriōs mitteret.
mitteret: 3rd sing imperfect subj act, FCC. mīsī: 1st sing perfect indicative act; note that the FCC clause expresses what was sent to the brother: The idea of an order be given is to be understood ("I sent <an order> that …").
28
tōtam hiemem ipse ad exercitum manēre dēcrēvit.
manēre: Present Infinitive Active, ICC. tōtam hiemem: ACC of extent of time. Because the verb manēre does not indicate motion, we may translate ad exercitum as "near the army."
29
persvāsit Lacedaimoniis, ut bellum rēgī facerent.
facerent: 3rd plural imperfect subj act, FCC. rēgī: the DAT here can be translated "against the king." For interpretation of the DAT Lacedaimoniis, see the verb frame of persvāsit in the vocab.
30
ut id quam saepissimē faciās, tē vehementer rogō.
faciās: 2nd sing present subj act, FCC. rogō: 1st sing present indicative act. Idiomatically, quam saepissimē = "as often as possible."
31
Indutiomarum hortātus est, utī in officiō manēret.
manēret: 3rd sing imperfect subj act, FCC. For hortātus est see #14.
32
mandat, ut crēbrōs explōrātōrēs in Svebōs mittant.
mittant: 3rd plur present subj act, FCC.
33
rēgem hortātus est, ut lēgātōs ad Achillam mitteret.
mitteret: 3rd sing imperfect subj act, FCC. For hortātus est see #14.
34
magnō opere ā mē petīvit, ut Lāodicēam prōtinus īrem.
īrem: 1st sing imperfect subj act of eō ("to go"), FCC. Lāodicēam: ACC of proper name of a city. For meaning of ā mē, see the verb frame info in the vocab.
35
placitum est mihi, ut Brundisium dūcerem hanc cōpiam.
dūcerem: 1st sing imperfect subj act, FCC. Brundisium: ACC of proper name of a city. placitum est = placuit ("it was pleasing").
36
scrībit Labiēnō, cum legiōne ad fīnīs Nervōrum veniat.
veniat: 3rd sing present subj act, FCC.
37
Ubiīs imperat, ut sua omnia ex agrīs in oppida cōnferant.
cōnferant: 3rd plural present subj act, FCC. For the DAT Ubiīs, see verb frame in vocab.
38
placuit nōbīs, ut statim ad Cornūtum litterās dēferrēmus.
dēferrēmus: 1st plur imperfect subj act, FCC.
39
eōdem ut sequerentur, ad Tīmotheum et Īphicraten nūntium mīsit.
sequerentur: 3rd plural imperfect subj deponent.
