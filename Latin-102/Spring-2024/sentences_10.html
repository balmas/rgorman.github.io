Latin 102
Active and Passive Periphrastics

1
respondī Clōdiō mē ita esse factūrum.
  • Clōdiō: PrN of a Roman, Clōdius, Clōdī, m.
  • mē: SBJ of infin.
  • esse factūrum: = infin with prospective aspect
2
sed ista rēs nūllō modō relinquenda est.
  • modō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by/by means of X"
  • relinquenda est: debitive/deontic aspect
3
cum Antōniō item est agendum, ut cum Cūriōne.
est agendum has debitive/deontic aspect and present tense.
4
cupiō scīre, quid agās et ubi sīs hiemātūrus.
sīs hiemātūrus has prospective aspect and present tense.
5
hanc putāvī meā manū scrībendam, itaque fēcī.
scrībendam is equivalent to scrībendam esse; it has debitive/deontic aspect.
6
dē frātre, ubi eum vīsūrī essēmus, nesciēbāmus.
vīsūrī essēmus has prospective aspect and past tense.
7
scrībis tē nescīre, quid nostrīs faciendum sit.
faciendum sit has debitive/deontic aspect and present tense. nostrīs is DAT of agent meaning "by our [men]"
8
putō enim etiam atque etiam mihi dīcendum esse.
dīcendum esse has debitive/deontic aspect. etiam atque etiam is idiomatic: "again and again."
9
illud quidem nec faciendum est nec fierī potest.
faciendum est has debitive/deontic aspect and present tense.
10
in eōs, quī haec ēgerunt, impetum factūrus vidētur.
factūrus is equivalent to factūrus esse.
11
prīdiē Kalendās Sextīlis putō mē Lāodicēae fore.
fore is equivalent of futūrum esse; it has prospective aspect. Kalendās Sextīlis is ACC of limit: "to the July Kalends."
12
nōn ferunt hominēs, quod vidētur esse tamen ferendum.
esse ferendum has debitive/deontic aspect.
13
erat enim quīntō Nōnās ventūrus vesperī, id est hodiē.
erat ventūrus has prospective aspect and past tense. Nōnās is ACC of limit: "to the Nones."
14
illī nōndum vēnerant sed erant ventūrī inānēs, imparātī.
erant ventūrī has prospective aspect and past tense. inānēs and imparātī are secondary predications.
15
quod dictūrus sum, putō equidem nōn valdē ad rem pertinēre.
dictūrus sum has prospective aspect and present tense. quod introduces a autonomous relative clause that is the SBJ of pertinēre.
16
ex tuīs litterīs cognōvī Pompēium nostrum in Hispāniam itūrum.
itūrum is equivalent of itūrum esse; it has prospective aspect.
