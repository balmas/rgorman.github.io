Latin 102
Participial Clauses 2

1
hōc quoque interfectō, exercitus summam imperī ad eum dētulit.
  • quoque: advert = "also"
  • summam: as a substantive = "supreme command"
2
ego tamen, Philotimī litterīs lēctīs, mūtāvī cōnsilium dē mulieribus.
  • Philotimī: PrN of a Roman, Philotimus, Philotimī, m.
  • lēctīs: = "read"
  • cōnsilium: = "plan"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
Philotimus is the name of a certain Greek man.
3
iam prope hieme cōnfectā, lēgātī ad eum prīncipēs Aeduōrum veniunt.
Aeduī is the name of a certain people. lēgātī is best understood as a secondary predication.
4
eō celeriter cōnfectō negōtiō, rūrsus in hīberna legiōnēs redūxit.
  • rūrsus: adverb = "back"
  • hīberna: = "winter quarters"
5
hāc pugnā nūntiātā, lēgātōs ad Caesarem mīsērunt sēque eī dēdidērunt.
  • sē ... dēdidērunt: = "surrendered themselves"
  • -que: Xque = "and X"
6
actīs hīs rēbus, litterae ā tē mihi redditae sunt, quibus ā mē cōnsilium petis.
  • actīs: = "(having been) done"
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • redditae sunt: = "was delilvered"
  • quibus: see relative adjective patterns; ABL(X) without prep. can = "by/by means of X"
7
nūllam partem noctis itinere intermissō, in fīnis Lingonum diē quārtō pervēnērunt.
  • partem: ACC(X) without prep. can = "for/for the duration of X"
  • Lingonum: PrN of a Gallic people, Lingonēs, Lingonum, m.
  • diē: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
8
quibus rēbus cognitīs, mediā nocte silentiō profectus ad hostium castra mane pervēnit.
  • quibus: a relative adjective may sometimes function as a simple anaphoric modifier, without subordinating force; = "these/those"
  • nocte: ABL(X) without prep. may = "at/in/on X"
  • profectus: perfect deponent in participial clause = "having + EN(verb)"
  • silentiō: adverb
  • mane: adverb
9
itaque duābus cohortibus Ilerdae praesidiō relictīs, omnibus cōpiīs Sicorim trānseunt.
  • Ilerdae: PrN of a town in Spain, Ilerda, Ilerdae, f.
  • praesidiō: DAT(X) can = "for/for the purpose of an X"
  • cōpiīs: ABL(X) without prep. can = "with/accompanied by X"
  • Sicorim: PrN of a River in Spain, Sicoris, Sicoris, m.
10
hāc ōrātiōne ab Dīviciācō habitā, omnēs, quī aderant, auxilium ā Caesare petere coepērunt.
Dīviciācus is the name of a certain Gaulish man.
11
hōc proeliō trāns Rhēnum nūntiātō, Svebī, quī ad rīpās Rhēnī vēnerant, domum revertī coepit.
Svebī is the name of a certain people. Rhēnus is the name of a famous river.
12
acceptīs mandātīs, Rōscius cum Caesare Capuam pervēnit ibique cōnsulēs Pompeiumque invēnit.
Rōscius is the name of a Roman man. Capua is the name of a certain city in Italy.
13
Germānī mūnītiōnēs nostrās ēgressī, complūribus interfectīs, sēsē ad suōs incolumēs recēpērunt.
incolumēs is best taken as a secondary predication.
14
Cūriō cum omnibus cōpiīs quārtā vigiliā exierat, cohortibus quīnque castrīs praesidiō relictīs.
Cūriō is the name of a Roman man. praesidiō: DAT of purpose as secondary predication.
15
hīs rēbus celeriter administrātis, ipse, cum prīmum per annī tempus potuit, ad exercitum contendit.
cum prīmum together can be translated "as soon as ..."
16
nūllō hoste prohibente, incolumem legiōnem in Nantuātēs, inde in Allobrogēs perdūxit ibique hiemāvit.
Nantuātēs and Allobrogēs are the names of peoples. incolumem is best undertood as a secondary publication.
17
iīs rēbus cognitīs ā captīvīs perfugīsque, Caesar, praemissō equitātū, cōnfestim legiōnēs subsequī iussit.
Several verbs, but not too difficult.
