Latin 102
Indirect Question Clauses

1
videō, quid ēgerim.
  • quid: interrogative pronoun.
2
videō, quid agat.
  • quid: interrogative pronoun.
3
scrīpsit, quid fierī vellet.
  • quid: interrogative pronoun; SBJ of infin.
  • fierī: infin. = "to be done"
4
nunc, quae sint negōtia, vidēs.
  • quae: interrrogative adjective (neuter plural nominative) = "what?"
5
sciō, quam sīs amīcīs iūcundus.
  • quam: interrogative adverb = "how"; quam is an adverb of degree as in English "how fast?" "how many?" etc.
6
vident, quantum in tē sit animī.
  • quantum: interrogative adjective = "how much?"
7
quaesīvit, unde esset epistula.
unde is an interrogative adverb and indicates a constituent indirect question.
8
quō quidem in cāsū sīmus, vidēs.
quō is an interrogative adjective and indicates a constituent indirect question.
9
prīmum quaerō, quī id scīre potuerit.
quī is not the interrogative pronoun "who"; it is the an interrogative adverb meaning "how." It indicates a constituent indirect question.
10
factum sit necne, vehementer quaeritur.
necne contains the interrogative particle -ne; it indicates a disjunctive clausal indirect question meaning "whether ... or not."
11
vidētis, quōs et quam multōs habeat Antōnius.
quōs id an interrogative pronoun and quam is an iterrogative adverb of degree: the introduce an constituent indirect question.
12
quanta multitūdō hominum convēnerit ad hoc iūdicium, vidēs.
quanta is an interrogative adjective and introduces an constituent indirect question.
13
nē dē Persārum quidem rēge magnō potes dīcere, beātusne sit.
nē ... quidem are focus particles; translate "not ... even." -ne is an enclitic interrogative particle. It introduces a clausal question focused on the word to which the enclitic is attached.
14
dē hōc prīmum quaerō, vēnerit eā rēs in hoc iūdicium necne.
necne contains the interrogative particle -ne; it indicates a disjunctive clausal indirect question meaning "whether ... or not."
15
equitēs ā Fabiō missī, quantō rēs in perīculō fuerit, expōnunt.
quantō is an interrogative adjective and indicates a constituent indirect question.
16
etiam quaerēbant, cūr ego ex ipsō cursū tam subitō revertissem.
cūr is an iterrogative adverb and indicates a constituent indirect question.
17
nunc quaerō, utrum vestrās iniūriās an reī pūblicae persequāminī.
utrum ... an are interrogative particles and indicate a disjunctive clausal question. They can be translated "whether ... or" or "... or."
