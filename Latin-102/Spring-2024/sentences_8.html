Latin 102
Indirect Statement Clauses

1
id tū eum nōlle mihi scrīpsistī.
  • eum: SBJ of the infinitive
2
sciō enim tē familiārem esse Othōnum.
  • tē: SBJ of the infinitive
  • familiārem: = "friend"
  • esse: verb frame: SBJ is PNOM(X), where X is same case (and gender and number if possible) of SBJ
  • Othōnum: PrN of members of a Roman family, Othōnēs, Othōnum, m. plural
3
Gaium Coelium quaestōrem hūc venīre audīstī.
Gaius Coelius is the name of a certain Roman man.
4
potuisse certē tenērī illam prōvinciam sciō.
Remember complementary infinitives are almost always present; indirect statement infinitives can be present or perfect.
5
sciō enim mē nihil tibi grātius facere posse.
possum almost always has a dependent infinitive, as here.
6
ad Pompēium contendit, ut adesse Caesarem nūntiāret.
contendit usually means "hastens" in Caesar. ut ... nūntiāret can is a purpose clause; translate "so that ..."
7
senātōrēs multōs esse Rōmae nōs quoque audierāmus.
quoque focuses emphasis on the preceding word: "we too."
8
eō ipsō diē Syrācūsae ā Mārcellō captae esse dīcuntur.
Syrācūsae is a pluralia tantum (it only occurs in the plural). Because dīcuntur is passive, the perfect passive infinitive agrees in gender, number, and case with Syrācūsae. Mārcellus is the name of a certain Roman man. ā Mārcellō expresses the agent of the verb ("by Marcellus").
9
Pompēium scīs temporibus illīs nōn saepe in senātū fuisse.
Pompēius is the name of a famous Roman general.
10
tunc ducēs prīncipēsque Nerviōrum colloquī sēsē velle dīcunt.
Nerviī is the name of a Gallic people.
11
Gaius Caesar inimīcissimus esse meae salūtī dīcēbātur.
A person is inimīcissimus to a DAT (here meae salūtī). Because dīcēbātur is passive, inimīcissimus agrees in gender, number and case with the subject of dīcēbātur.
12
litterīs scrīptīs, audīvī Dolābellam in Ciliciam vēnisse cum suīs cōpiīs.
Dolābella is the name of a certain Roman man. Cilicia is the name of a place.
13
audierat Pompēium per Maurētāniam cum legiōnibus iter in Hispāniam facere.
Maurētānia is the name of a region in Africa. Hispānia is the name of a place (roughly modern Spain and Portugal).
14
illud quidem perlibenter audīvī ex eōdem Chrȳsippō, tē esse Caesarī familiārem.
perlibenter means "very gladly." Chrȳsippus is the name of a certain Greek man. familiāris here means "friendly."
15
intellēxī ex tuīs litterīs tē ex Turrāniō audīsse ā mē prōvinciam frātrī trāditam.
Turrānius is the name of a cerain Roman man. Here trāditam is the functional equivalent of trāditam esse.
16
Hirtius ad mē scrīpsit Sextum Pompēium Cordubā exīsse et fūgisse in Hispāniam citeriōrem.
Hirtius is the name of a certain Roman man. Sextus Pompēius is the son of the famous Roman general. Corduba is the name of a city. Hispānia is the name of a region.
17
periūcundae mihi fuērunt litterae tuae, quibus intellēxī tē perspicere meam in tē pietātem.
periūcundae means "very pleasing." pietās can mean "loyalty" directed to one's family or close friends.
