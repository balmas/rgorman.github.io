Relative Pronouns

1
legiōnēs, quās sēcum habēbat, contrā hostīs dūxit.
In-class demo.
2
mīlitēs, quōs invenīre potuit, in ūnum locum condūxit.
In-class demo.
3
mīlitēs, quī captī erant, effūgērunt.
In-class demo.
4
animālia, quae capta erant, effūgērunt.
In-class demo.
5
accēpī fasciculum, in quō erat epistula Piliae.
In-class demo.
6
lēgātī ab hīs vēnērunt, quōrum haec fuit ōrātiō.
In-class demo.
7
venit ad mē, quod mihi pergrātum fuit, noster Hortēnsius.
In-class demo.
8
nunc veniō ad eam epistulam, quam accēpī ā Tulliō.
9
sed haec Tīrōnī mandāvī, quem ob eam causam Rōmam mīsī.
Tīrō is the personal name of a certain man; it is DAT here.
10
litterārum exempla, quās ad Pompeium scrīpsī, mīsī tibi.
Pompeius is the proper name of a certain man; it is ACC here.
11
dē hīs eandem ferē, quam reliquae gentēs, habent opīniōnem.
ferē is a kind of adverb sometimes called an Intensifier. This term includes adverbs that strengthen another word such as "very" but also adverbs that weakens (for example, "almost"). Intensifiers are frequently dependent on adjectives and other adverbs and not only on verbs.
12
postrīdiē senātūs cōnsultum factum est id, quod ad tē mīsī.
senātūs cōnsultum is a technical term: "a decree of the Senate."
13
is inter Thrasybūlum et eōs, quī urbem tenēbant, fēcit pācem.
Thrasybūlus is the proper name of a famous Athenian general/politician; it is ACC here.
14
eō Lūcius Caesar vēnit, cuius pater Caesaris erat lēgātus.
Lūcius Caesar is the proper name of a relative of the famous Caesar; it is NOM here.
15
Decimus Tullius, cui mandāta ad mē dedistī, nōn convēnerat mē.
Decimus Tullius is the proper name of a certain Roman man; it is NOM here.
16
Iccius Remus, quī tum oppidō praefuerat, nūntium ad eum mittit.
Iccius is the proper name of Gallic man; it is NOM here. Remus is an adjective referring to a Gallic tribe, the Remi. Remus can be translated a/the Reman. The relevant frame for praesum is "SBJ is in charge of DAT."
17
dē Terentiā autem scrīpsī ad tē iīs litterīs, quās dederam prīdiē.
Terentiā is the proper name of a certain Roman woman; it is ABL here.
18
quārtae aciēī, quam īnstituerat ex cohortium numerō, dēdit signum.
For the meaning of īnstituerat, see definition II.F. aciēī is a "line of battle."
19
praefuit paucīs nāvibus, quās ex Syriā iussus erat in Asiam dūcere.
The relevant frame for praesum is "SBJ is in charge of DAT."
20
itaque scrīpsī ad eum accūrātē, cuius epistulae mīsī ad tē exemplum.
Note that cuius and epistulae are not the same gender. What do you think this indicates?
21
cōnsulēs, quod ante id tempus accidit numquam, ex urbe proficīscuntur.
quod indicates that this relative clause specifies the whole main clause.
22
dē adventū Caesaris idem, quod ā tē, mihi scrīptum est ab Oppiō et Balbō.
Oppius and Balbus are names of certain Roman men; they are ABL here.
23
Cūriōnem cum tribus cohortibus, quās Pisaurī et Arīminī habēbat, mittit.
Cūriō is the proper name of a certain Roman man; it is ACC here. Pisaurum and Arīminum are proper names of certain Italian towns.
24
Gnaeus Pompeius fīlius, quī classī Aegyptiae praeerat, ad Ōricum venit.
Gnaeus Pompeius fīlius = "Gnaeus Pompey Jr"; the name is NOM here.
25
ego omnīnō Capuam venī eō ipsō diē, quō tū Teānō Sidicīnō es profectus.
Capua is the proper name of a famous Italian city; it is ACC here. Teānum Sidicīnum is the proper name of a certain Italian town; it is ABL here.
26
nam equitātuī, quem auxiliō Caesarī Aeduī miserant, Dumnorix praeerat.
Aeduī is the name of a Gallic tribe. Dumnorix is the proper name of a certain Gallic man; it is NOM here.
27
hoc tamen flūmen in eā parte erat urbis, quae ab Alexandrīnīs tenēbātur.
Alexandrīnus refers to a resident of the Egyptian city of Alexandria; it is ABL here.
28
Gāius Cōpōnius, quī Dyrrachī classī Rhodiae praeerat, nāvīs ex portū ēdūcit.
Gāius Cōpōnius is the proper name of a Roman man; it is NOM here. Dyrrachium is the name of a town. Rhodius, -a, -um is an ADJ referring to the island of Rhodes.
29
prōtinus omnēs in altissimōs montīs, quī ad castra pertinēbant, cōnfūgērunt.
Nothing too noteworthy here.
30
Lūcium Pīsōnem lēgātum Tigurīnī eōdem proeliō, quō Cassium, interfēcerant.
Lūcius Pīsō is the proper name of a certain Roman man; it is ACC here. Cassius is ditto.
31
spem maximam habeō in Balbō, ad quem dē tē dīligentissimē et saepissimē scrībō.
Balbus is the proper name of a certain Roman man; it is ABL here.
32
Accō, quī prīnceps eius cōnsilī fuerat, iubet in oppida multitūdinem convenīre.
Accō is the proper name of a certain Gallic man; it is NOM here.
33
Balbī Cornēlī litterārum exemplum, quās eōdem diē accēpī, quō tuās, mīsī ad tē.
Balbus Cornēlius is the proper name of a certain Roman man; it is GEN here.
34
Nōnīs accēpī ā tē duās epistulās, quārum alteram Kalendīs dederās, alteram prīdiē.
Nōnae is the name of a certain day of the month according to the Roman calendar; it is ABL here. Ditto for Kalendae. Translate "the Nones" and "the Kalends."
35
Ubiī, quī ante obsidēs dederant atque in dēditiōnem vēnerant, ad eum lēgātōs mittunt.
Ubius refers to a cerain Germanic tribe; it is NOM here. It can be translated "the Ubians."
36
castra paucōs diēs habuimus ea ipsa, quae contrā Dārīum habuerat apud Issum Alexander.
paucōs diēs is an Extent construction: "for a few days." Dārīus is the proper name of a famous Persian king; it is ACC here. Issus is the proper name of a town in Asia; it is ACC here. Alexander is the proper name of a famous general; it is NOM here.
37
ipse ad flūmen Scaldim, quod īnfluit in Mosam, extrēmāsque Arduennae partīs īre cōnstituit.
Scaldis is the proper name of a certain river (today the Scheldt); it is ACC here. Ditto for Mosa (today the Meuse/Maas). Arduenna is the proper name of a ceratin forest; it is GEN.
38
Achillās occupābat Alexandrēam praeter eam oppidī partem, quam Caesar cum mīlitibus tenēbat.
Achillās is the proper name of a certain Egyptian man; it is NOM here. Alexandrēa is the proper name of a famous city; it is ACC here.
39
mittuntur etiam ad eās cīvitātēs lēgātī, quae sunt citeriōris Hispāniae fīnitimae.
Hispānia is the proper name of a Roman province; it is GEN here.
40
Caesar ad oppidum Avāricum, quod erat maximum mūnītissimumque in fīnibus Biturigum, profectus est.
Avāricum is the proper name of a town in Gaul; it is ACC here. Biturigēs refers to a Gallic tribe; it is GEN here and can be translated "the Bituriges."
41
īdem praetor fuit Athēniēnsium apud Plataeās in proeliō, quō barbarōrum exercitus interfectus est.
Athēniēnsēs refers to citizens of Athens; it is GEN here and can be translated "the Athenians." Plataeae is the proper name of a certain town in Greece, the location of a famous battle; it is ACC here.
