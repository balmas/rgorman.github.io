Latin 102
Temporal Relatives, Temporal clauses, etc.

1
cum Philogenēs Lāodicēam ad mē vēnisset, hās litterās dedī.
  • cum: suboridnating conjunction, = "since/when"
  • Philogenēs: PrN of a Greek man, Philogenēs, Philogenis, m.
  • Lāodicēam: PrN of a city in Asia Minor, Lāodicēa, Lāodicēae, f.
2
illīus temporis, dum in spē pāx fuit, ratiōnem nostram vidēs.
  • dum: subordinator, = "while"
  • ratiōnem: probably = "system/plan"
3
cum Brundisium versus īrēs ad Caesarem, vēnistī ad mē in Formiānum.
  • cum: subordinator = "since/when"
  • Brundisium: PrN of an Italian town, Brundisium, Brundisī, n.; ACC(X) without a prep. can = "to X"
  • versus: adverb, = "towards," usually placed after the word it specifies
  • Formiānum: PrN of a country estate, Formiānum, Formiānī, n.
4
cum ad tē litterās dedissem, redditae mihi sunt litterae ā Pompeiō.
  • cum: subordinator = "since/when"
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • Pompeiō: PrN of a Roman man, Pompeius, Pompeī, m.
5
Servius cum esset apud mē, Cephaliō cum tuīs litterīs sextō Īdūs vēnit.
  • Servius: PrN of a Roman, Servius, Servī, m.
  • cum: subordinator = "since/when"
  • apud: prep. + ACC(X) = "at X's house"
  • Cephaliō: PrN of an enslaved person, Cephaliō, Cephaliōnis, m.
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
  • sextō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • Īdūs: ACC(X) without prep. can = "to/before X"
6
nam cum ad tē sextō Nōnās darem, eīdem tabellāriō dedī etiam ad Sestium.
  • cum: subordinator = "since/when"
  • sextō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • Nōnās: ACC(X) without prep. can = "to/before X"
  • eīdem: see adjectives 3, = "the same"
  • etiam: adverb = "also"
  • Sestium: PrN of a Roman, Sestius, Sestī, m.
7
tulit haec cīvitās, quoad necesse fuit, vestram dominātiōnem.
  • quoad: subordinator = "while/as long as"
8
in Cūmānō cum essem, vēnit ad mē, quod mihi pergrātum fuit, noster Hortēnsius.
  • Cūmānō: PrN of a country estate, Cūmānum, Cūmānī, n.
  • cum: subordinator = "when/since"
  • quod: see relative adjective patterns; quod refers to the entire situation described in the matrix clause
  • Hortēnsius: PrN of a Roman person, Hortēnsius, Hortēnsī, m.
9
Tūberō cum in Āfricam vēnisset, invēnit in prōvinciā cum imperiō Attium Vārum.
  • Tūberō: PrN of a Roman person, Tūberō, Tūberōnis, m.
  • cum: subordinator = "when/since"
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
  • Attium Vārum: PrN of a Roman person, Attius Vārus, Attī Vārī, m.
10
ubi hostēs ad lēgātōs exercitumque pervēnērunt, ūniversī sē ad pedēs prōiciunt.
  • ubi: relative adverb = "when"
  • ūniversī: rather emphatic = "one and all"
  • pedēs: = "[their] feet"
11
sed ubi signa legiōnum appropinquāre coepērunt, sēsē in proximōs montīs cōnferunt.
  • ubi: relative adverb = "when"
  • signa: = "standards/emblems"
12
cum Caesar in Galliam vēnit, alterīus factiōnis prīncipēs erant Aeduī, alterīus Sēquanī.
  • cum: subordinator = "when"
  • Aeduī: PrN of a Gallic people, Aeduī, Aeduōrum, m. plural
  • Sēquanī: PrN of a Gallic people, Sēquanī, Sēquanōrum, m. plural
13
cum Lūcius Metellus in prōvinciam profectus est, tum istī cum Metellō ūnā proficīscuntur.
  • cum: subordinator = "when"
  • Lūcius Metellus: PrN of a Roman, Lūcius Metellus, Lūcī Metellī, m.
  • cum: prep. + ABL(X) = "with X"
  • Metellō: PrN of a Roman, Metellus, Metellī, m.
  • ūnā: adverb - "together/at the same time"
14
in Pīraeō, cum exīssem prīdiē Īdūs Octōbrīs, accēpī ab Acastō, servō meō, statim tuās litterās.
  • Pīraeō: PrN of a Greek town, the port of Athens, Pīraeus, Pīraeī, m.
  • cum: subordinator = "when"
  • Īdūs: ACC(X) without prep. can = "to/before X"
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
15
nam cum in Tusculānum vesperī vēnissem, postrīdiē ille ad mē, nōndum firmō corpore cum esset, māne vēnit.
  • cum: subordinator = "when"
  • Tusculānum: PrN of a country estate, Tusculānum, Tusculānī, n.
  • vesperī: adverb = "in the evening"
  • nōndum: adverb, may specify an adjective; nōndum firmō = "not yet healthy"
  • corpore: ABL(X) without prep. may = "with X" and indivate manner or general characteristic
  • māne: adverb = "in the morning"
16
iter Lāodicēā faciēbam tertiō Nōnās Sextīlīs, cum hās litterās dabam, in castra in Lycāoniam.
  • Lāodicēā: PrN of a town in Asia Minor, Lāodicēa, Lāodicēae, f. ABL(X) without prep. can = "from X"
  • tertiō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • Nōnās: ACC(X) without prep. can = "before/to X"
  • cum: subordinator = "when"
  • Lycāoniam: PrN of a region in Asia Minor, Lycāonia, Lycāoniae, f.
17
commodum discesserat Hilarus quārtō Kalendās, cui dederam litterās ad tē, cum vēnit tabellārius cum tuīs litterīs.
  • commodum: adverb = "just before that time"
  • Hilarus: PrN of a Roman, Hilarus, Hilarī, m.
  • quārtō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • Kalendās" ACC(X) without prep. can = "before/to X"
  • cui: see relative adjective patterns
  • cum: subordinator = "when"
Hilarus was the name of a certain Roman man.
