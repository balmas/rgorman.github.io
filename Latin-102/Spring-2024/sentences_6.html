Latin 102
Participial Clauses 1

1
hoc spērāns legiōnēs trīs ex castrīs ēdūcit.
  • trīs: = "three," trēs, tria; see adjectives 2
2
Caelius profectus ad Caesarem pervēnit Thūriōs.
  • Caelius: PrN of a Roman, Caelius, Caelī, m.
  • profectus: perfect deponents in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
  • Thūriōs: PrN of a town in Italy, Thūriī, Thūriōrum, m. plural
3
heri dederam ad tē litterās exiēns ē Puteolānō.
  • ē: prep. + ABL(X) = "out of/from X"
  • Puteolānō: PrN of a country estate in Italy, Puteolānum, Puteolānī, n.
4
prīdiē Īdūs Fundīs accēpī tuās litterās cēnāns.
  • Īdūs: ACC(X) without prep. can = "to/before X"
  • Fundīs: PrN of a town in Italy, Fundī, Fundōrum, m. plural; ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
5
hās tibi litterās exiēns in Tusculānum scrīpsī.
  • Tusculānum: PrN of a country estate in Italy, Tusculānum, Tusculānī, n.
6
inde per Ligurēs Appennīnum trānsiit petēns Etrūriam.
  • Ligurēs: PrN of an Italian people, Ligurēs, Ligurum, m. plural
  • Appennīnum: PrN of a mountain range in Italy, Appennīnus, Appennīnī, m. = "the Appenines"
  • Etrūriam: PrN of a region in Italy, Etrūria, Etrūriae, f.
7
Pompeius idōneum locum nactus ibi cōpiās collocāvit.
  • nactus: perfect deponents in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
8
itinera fēcit multō maiōra fugiēns quam ego sequēns.
  • itinera: can = the distance travelled in one day = "a day's march"
  • multō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "by X" as in, for example, "taller by a foot"
  • quam: subordinator introducing a comparison clause, = "than"
9
tamen cōnscendēns iam nāvem Ephesō Lāodicēam revertit.
  • cōnscendēns: = "going aboard"
  • Ephesō: PrN of a Greek city in Asia Minor, Ephesus, Ephesī, f. ABL(X) without prep. can = "from X"
  • Lāodicēam: PrN of a town in Asia Minor, Lāodicēa, Lāodicēae, f.
10
reliquōs omnīs equitātū cōnsecūtī nostrī interfēcērunt.
  • equitātū: ABL(X) without prep. can = "with X"
  • cōnsecūtī: perfect deponent in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
11
quīntō Kalendās mane accēpī ā Dēmeā litterās prīdiē datās.
  • quīntō: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • Kalendās: ACC(X) without prep. can = "to/before X"
  • mane: adverb = "in the morning"
  • ā: prep. + ABL(X) = "from X"
  • Dēmeā: PrN of an enslaved person, Dēmea, Dēmeae, m.
12
ēgressus inde, diēs noctēsque iter faciēns Taurum trānsiit.
  • ēgressus: perfect deponent in participial clauses = "having + -EN(verb)"
  • diēs: ACC(X) without prep. can = "for/for the duration of X"
  • noctēs: ACC(X) without prep. can = "for/for the duration of X"
  • Taurum: PrN of a mountain range in Asia Minor, Taurus, Taurī, m.
13
prīnceps decima legiō per tribūnōs mīlitum eī grātiās ēgit.
  • prīnceps: a predicative use of an adjective; this function is called a secondary predication.
  • decima: = "tenth," decimus, decima, decimum
  • grātiās ēgit: grātiās agō = "I thank"; the person being thanked is in the DAT
14
eōdem diē lēgātī ab hostibus missī ad Caesarem dē pāce vēnērunt.
  • eōdem: = "the same," see adjectives 3
  • diē: ABL(X) without prep. can = "at/in/on X"
  • ab: prep. + ABL(X) = "by/through the agency of X"
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
15
cōnfēcit prior iter Caesar atque contrā hostem aciem īnstruit.
  • prior: a predicative use of an adjective; this function is called a secondary predication.
  • contrā: prep. + ACC(X) = "against X"
  • aciem: = "battle line"
16
tibi autem crēbrō ad mē scrībentī dē eius officiō nihil rescrīpsī.
Nothing too odd here.
17
multās ā tē accēpī epistulās eōdem diē omnīs dīligenter scrīptās.
omnīs is a secondary predication--thus omnīs and multās can both modify epistulās without leading to nonsense.
18
ipsī idōneum locum nactī reliquam noctis partem ibi cōnfēcērunt.
nactī is deponent: completed active [having + -EN(verb)]
19
ego dē prōvinciā dēcēdēns quaestōrem Coelium praeposuī prōvinciae.
Here is rare example of dē meaning "from." The meaning is only usual whe the verb contains the prefix dē-, as does dēcēdēns here. Coelius is the name of a certain Roman man.
20
quō nūntiō Artaxerxēs commōtus Autophradatem in Cappadociam mittit.
Artaxerxēs and Autophradates are names of certain men. Cappadocia is the name of a place. quō is a "non-relative" relative and can be translated "this."
21
nōn enim sumus omnīnō sine cūrā venientis annī, etsī sumus sine timōre.
cūrā venientis annī: we would say "care for the coming year."
22
proficīscēns enim ē Tusculānō octāvō Kalendās mīsī ad eum tabellāriōs.
Tusculānum is the name of a certain estate.
23
itaque cōnsilium mūtāvit et ad tē statim meā manū scrīptās litterās mīsī.
Nothing too note-worthy here.
24
ipse paulum ex eō locō cum legiōne prōgressus ēventum pugnae exspectābat.
paulum is adverbial meaning "a short distance."
25
dē senātūs cōnsultō certior factus dēlēctum tōtā prōvinciā habēre īnstituit.
certior factus is a common structure in Caesar, meaning "having been made more certain."
26
quibus rēbus commōtī lēgātī mīlitēs ex opere dēdūcunt, oppugnātiōne dēsistunt.
quibus a "non-relative" relative. Translate as "these."
27
Cenabensēs paulō ante mediam noctem ex oppidō ēgressī flūmen trānsīre coepērunt.
Cenabensēs the name of a certain Gaulish people.
28
interfectus est etiam fortissimē pugnāns Crāstinus, cuius mentiōnem suprā fēcimus.
Crāstinus is the name of a certain Roman man.
29
illī nōn diū commorātī nec longius ab īnfimō colle prōgressī cōpiās in castra redūcunt.
