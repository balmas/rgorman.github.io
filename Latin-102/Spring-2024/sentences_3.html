Latin 102
Relative Adjectives and Adverbs

1
dē Siccā ita est, ut scrībis.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "concerning/about X"
  • Siccā: PrN of a Roman man, Sicca, Siccae, m.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
2
dē Oppiō factum est, ut voluī.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • Oppiō: PrN of a Roman man, Oppius, Oppī, m.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
3
Basilus, ut imperātum est, facit.
  • Basilus: PrN of a Roman man, Basilus, Basilī, m.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
4
quā cōnstituerat, proficīscitur.
  • quā: relative adverb = "by which path/where
5
Domitius, ut audiō, in Cosānō est.
  • Domitius: PrN of a Roman man, Domitius, Domitī, m.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
  • in: prep. + ABL(X) = "in X"
  • Cosānō: PrN of a country estate near the town of Cosa, Cosānum, Cosānī, n.
6
dē Asiāticō itinere, ut imperāvistī, fēcī.
  • dē: prep. + ABL(X) = "about/concerning X"
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
7
quot hominum linguae, tot nōmina deōrum.
  • quot: a plural adjective; it is indeclinable (it has the same form for all genders and cases) = "how many/as many as"
  • tot: a plural adjective; it is indeclinable (it has the same form for all genders and cases) = "so many"
8
habuit suum negōtium Gallus, ut scrībit.
  • Gallus: PrN of a Roman man, Gallus, Gallī, m.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
9
ad Faberium, ut tibi placuit, litterās mīsī.
  • ad: prep. + ACC(X) = "to X"
  • Faberium: PrN of a Roman man, Faberius, Faberī, m.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
  • placuit: verb frame: SBJ is acceptable/pleasing to X, where "to X" is DAT
10
tantum erat, quantum hominēs loquēbantur.
  • tantum: = "so great/so large"
  • quantum: = "how great/large, as great/large as"
11
tālis in hāc urbe, quālis Aristīdēs Athēnīs fuit.
  • tālis: adjective = "that sort"; the verb of the main clause is not expressed
  • in: prep. + ABL(X) = "at/in/on X"
  • quālis: relative adjective = "what/which sort"
  • Aristīdēs: PrN of a Greek man, Aristīdēs, Aristīdis, m.
  • Athēnīs: PrN of a city in Greece, Athēnae, Athēnārum, f. plural; ABL(X) without a prep. can = "at/in/on X"
12
capiō tantum voluptātis, quantum labōris.
  • capiō: in Latin one can "grab" pleasure, while in English we "take" it.
  • tantum voluptātis: = "so much of X"
  • quantum: relative adjective; the verb of the relative clause is not expressed; = "how much (of Y)"
13
quō contenderat, pervenit iīsque locīs castra pōnit.
  • quō: relative adverb = "whither"
  • contenderat: = "hurry/make haste"
  • -que: coordinating conjunction, Xque = "and X"
  • locīs: ABL(X) without a prep. can = "at/in/on X"
14
nōluit eum locum, unde Helvētiī discesserant, vacāre.
  • unde: relative adverb = "whence"
  • Helvētiī: PrN of a Gallic people, Helvētiī, Helvētiōrum, m. plural
15
Caesar, ut cōnstituerat, in Ītaliam proficīscitur.
  • ut: relative adverb = "as"
  • in: prep. + ACC(X) = "into X"
16
omnēs Uticam relinquunt et, quō imperātum est, trānseunt.
  • Uticam: PrN of a town in Africa, Utica, Uticae, f.
  • quō: relative adverb = "whither"
