Sentence 1

    Caesar has won his battles.
    The enemy send ambassadors to negotiate.
    Among them is a familiar face.

  1. Una cum his legatis Commius Atrebas venit , quem supra demonstraveram a Caesare in Britanniam praemissum .

    2. The main verb and its simple sub-trees.
    • Note that una is an adverb; it is often further specified by and adverbial prepositional phrase.
    • Note that Atrebas may be considered an adjective giving the nationality of a person; compare the English use of "German."
    • Note finally that a complex ATR sub-tree gives further information about one of the elements.

  2. Una cum his legatis Commius Atrebas venit [ATR]

    3. The comples ATR sub-tree.
    • There are two verbs in this sub-tree. What kind of clauses are they?
      • Note that demonstraveram is introduced by quem. What does that tell you about the kind of clause>
      • Note that praemissum can be considered short for praemissum esse. That makes it an infinitive clause. What kind is it?
    • Note that an agent construction such as a Caesare is considered a kind of pseudo-argument; it would be a subject if the verb were active voice. Thus is it labeled as OBJ so that the argument structure of the verb can be roconstructed more easily.

  3. [[Commius]] quem supra demonstraveram a Caesare in Britanniam praemissum

    4. Put the whole sentence together.

  4. Una cum his legatis Commius Atrebas venit , quem supra demonstraveram a Caesare in Britanniam praemissum .

Sentence 2

    Caesar has met the enemy ambassadors. They pass on his peace terms to their people.
    • Note that there are coordinated main verbs, each at the head of a complex sub-tree. It is best to do one at a time.

  1. Interea suos in agros remigrare iusserunt , principes -que undique convenire et se civitates -que suas Caesari commendare coeperunt .

    2. The first main verb.
    • Note that iusserunt is anaphoric. Its subject is legati ("ambassadors") froma the previous context.
    • What kind of infinitive clause is remigrare

  2. Interea suos in agros remigrare iusserunt

    3. The second main clause and its dependencies.
    • Note that there are coordinated elements. Be careful with them.
    • Note that in this context commendare can be translated as "hand over."
    • Note also that commendare takes and accusative OBJ of the thing handed over and a dative OBJ of the person it is handed over to.
    • What kind of infinitive clauses are convenire and commendare?

  3. Interea principes undique convenire et se civitates -que suas Caesari commendare coeperunt

    4. Now put the whole thing together.

  4. Interea suos in agros remigrare iusserunt , principes -que undique convenire et se civitates -que suas Caesari commendare coeperunt .
