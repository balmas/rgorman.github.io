Latin 301
Imperative and Other Volitives

1
faciāmus igitur, ut cēnsēs.
In a main clause when there is no subordinate conditional clasue (or equivalent) a PRES SUBJ will usually indicate volitive: faciāmus = "let us ..." The relative ut can be translated "as."
2
utinam semper essēs tribūnus.
The adverb utinam usually indicates as wish. Here essēs is present unreal. We can translate "if only ..."
3
cūrā, ut valeās et mē absentem dīligās et dēfendās.
The adjective absentem is here a secondary predication; it can be translated "in my absence."
4
haec etiam, sī vidēbitur, cum Terentiā loquere opportūnē.
Note that loquere is depoenent. videō in the passive can mean "seem like a good idea" (see our on-line lexicon at section II.B.7.c)
5
ad hās litterās statim mihi rescrībe tuōrumque aliquem mitte.
Fairly uncomplicated.
6
vidē, nē tua iam, mī Brūte, culpa futūra sit, sī ego quicquam timeam.
videō can mean "see to it that" (Lexicon: II.B.3)
7
dē Būthrōtiō negōtiō utinam quidem Antōnium conveniam.
utinam indicates that the sentence is a wish. conveniam is present volitive.
8
potestās modo veniendī in hunc locum sit et dīcendī perīculum nōn recūsō.
modo modulates the force of the volitive verb; translate "only/just let ..."
9
ēgredere ex urbe, Catilīna, līberā rem pūblicam metū, in exsilium proficīscere.
The 3 main verbs are coordinated by asyndeton. Catalina is a PN.
10
utinam illum diem videam, cum tibi agam grātiās, quod mē vīvere coēgistī.
utinam indicates that the sentence is a wish. videam is present volitive: "Only let me ..."
11
in quā nōlīte ā mē, quasi dubium sit aliquid, argūmenta, iūdicēs, exspectāre.
nōlīte: negative "commands" often use the imperative of nōlō + a complete clause ("do not be willing to ..."). sit is present potential.
12
tū quid agās, ubi sīs, cuius modī istae rēs sint, fac mē quam dīligentissimē certiōrem.
agās, sīs, and sint are verbs of indirect questions coordinated by asyndeton. quam + a superlative adjective or adverb meaning x can be translated "as x as possible."
13
dicātur sānē ēiectus ā mē, dum modo eat in exsilium.
dicātur is volitive. The expression dum modo can be translated "provided that..."
14
tū sī ūnō in locō es futūrus, crēbrās ā nōbīs litterās exspectā, sed plūrīs etiam ipse mittitō.
mittitō is so-called future imperative: "send" or "that shalt send."
15
ōrātiōnem Quīntī Celeris mihi velim mittās contrā Mārcum Servīlium, et litterās mitte quam prīmum.
Quintus Celer is a PN; likewise Marcus Servilius. quam prīmum is idiomatic "as soon as possible."
16
quae quidem sī potentia est appellanda, appellētur ita sānē, dum modo ea nōs ūtāmur prō salūte bonōrum.
est appellanda is debitive in aspect: "[subj] must be x," where x is the en-form of the verb. appellētur is a present subjunctive used with volitive sense. The expression dum modo can be translated "provided that."
17
nōlī putāre mē ad quemquam longiōrēs epistulās scrībere nisi sī quis ad mē plūra scrīpsit, cui putō rescrībī oportēre.
On the use of nōlī, see sentence 11. The redundancy in the sequence nisi sī is not unknown in informal English.
18
effice, sī mē dīligās, ut valeās et ut ad nōs firmus ac valēns quam prīmum veniās.
dīligās: political relationships are often spoken of as if they were personal (or even romantic) relationships. firmus and valēns are secondary predications. quam prīmum is idiomatic: "as soon as possible."
19
fīnem nūllum faciō, mihi crēde, Cassī, dē tē et Brūtō nostrō, id est dē tōtā rē pūblicā, cōgitandī, cuius omnis spēs in vōbīs est et Decimō Brūtō.
mihi crēde is parenthetic; i.e., it is independent of the structure of the matrix sentence. id est and its subordinate elements may be parenthetical or appositional. Decimus Brutus is a PN.
20
tū, mī Volumnī, quoniam et īnstituistī et mihi vidēs esse grātum, scrībe ad mē quam saepissimē dē rēbus urbānīs, dē rē pūblicā.
īnstituistī here probably means "proposed" (Def. II.H). quam saepissimē: quam + x where x is superlative means "as x as possible."
