Sentences 8

Translate these sentences into English and be ready to explain your work.

1
adhūc nōn vēnērunt.
  • vēnērunt: veniō, venīre, vēnī, ventum; the adverb adhūc indicates that the verb "looks back" on the past from the perspective of the present. Thus, vēnērunt should be translated as a completed present.
2
adhūc nōn est profectus.
  • est profectus: proficīscor, proficīscī, profectus est; the adverb adhūc indicates that the verb "looks back" on the past from the perspective of the present. Thus, est profectus should be translated as a completed present.
3

  • fīlius: masculine
  • vēnerat: veniō, venīre, vēnī, ventum
4

  • ea: see "Adjectives 3"
  • loca: neuter
  • erat dēductus: dēdūcō, dēdūcere, dēdūxī, dēductus
5

  • Alexandrī: PrN, Alexander, Alexandrī, m.
  • fuerant: sum, esse, fuī, ____
  • amīcī: masculine
6
haec causa est eius modī.
  • causa: feminine
  • modī: masculine; this sentence illustrates how a genitive noun is functionally an adjective and thus can be used as a PNOM.
7
heri enim vesperī vēnerat.
  • heri: adverb
  • enim: sentence adverb; generally enim indicates that the sentence containing it confirms/explains a previous statement or statements.
  • vesperi: adverb = "in the evening"
  • vēnerat: veniō, venīre, vēnī, ventum
8
mihi in mentem nōn vēnerat.
  • mihi: here mihi ... mentem seems to = meam mentem = "my mind"
  • mentem: feminine
  • vēnerat: veniō, venīre, vēnī, ventum
9
sunt Rōmae lēgātī Mīlēsiī.
  • sunt: verb frame: SBJ is PNOM where PNOM is usually the same case as SBJ
  • Rōmae: feminine, locative case; a locative noun can be the second argument of sum
  • lēgātī: masculine
  • Mīlēsiī: PrN of a Greek people, Mīlēsiī, Mīlēsiōrum, m. = "Milesians"
10
illī autem nōndum vēnerant.
  • autem: sentence adverb, indicates that the truth of this sentence is unexpected given whatever has preceded
  • vēnerant: veniō, venīre, vēnī, ventum
11
is tum rēgis rēbus praeerat.
  • is: see is, ea, id in "Adjectives 3"
  • rēgis: masculine
  • rēbus: feminine
  • praeerat: praesum, praeesse, praefuī, ____; verb frame: SBJ is in charge of DAT X
12
praesidiō hostium tenēbātur.
  • praesidiō: neuter; ABL X without a preposition can = "at/in/on X"
  • hostium: masculine, plural follows i-stem pattern
  • tenēbātur: teneō, tenēre, tenuī, tentus
