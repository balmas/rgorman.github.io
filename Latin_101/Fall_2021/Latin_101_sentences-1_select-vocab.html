Sentences 1
Vocabulary to be Memorized

ā, ăb, ābs, preposition + abl.: from (departure, cause, remote origin/time

ad, preposition + acc.: to, up to, towards

castra, -ōrum, n. pl.: camp, military camp/field (plural only)

classĭs, -is, f.: fleet;

cōpĭa, -ae, f.: forces;

cŭm, preposition + abl.: with

dē, preposition + abl.: about, of, concerning; with regard to;

dīscēdō, dīscēdere, discessī, dīscessum: withdraw;

dūcō, dūcere, dūxī, ductum: to lead

ē, (also appears as ex) preposition + abl.: out of, from

ēdūcō, ēdūcere edūxī, ēductum: to lead out;

ĕgō̆, meī, pron.: “I”

ĕō, adv.: there, to that place

ĕōdĕm, adv.: to the same place

ĕquĕs, equitis, m.: horseman/cavalryman/rider;

ēx, preposition + abl.: out of, from;

ēxērcĭtus, -ūs, m.: army, infantry;

fīlĭus, -ī, m.: son;

flūmĕn, flūminis, n.: river, stream;

hōstĭs, -is, m.: enemy (of the state);

ĭn, preposition + acc. or + abl.: in, into, on

indĕ, adv.: thence, thenceforth; from that place;

lēgātus, -ī, m.: commander of a legion; officer; deputy;

lĕgĭō, legiōnis, f.: legion; army;

līttĕra, -ae, f.: letter (alphabet); (pl.) letter, epistle;

mē, accusative singular or ablative singular of pronoun ego, “I,” “me,” “myself”

mīlĕs, mīlitis, m.: soldier; foot soldier;

mīttō, mīttere, mīsī, missum: to send

nōn, adv. neg.: not, by no means, no

nūntĭus, -ī, m.: messenger

ōppĭdŭm, -ī, n.: town;

pāx, pacis, f.: peace; harmony;

pervĕnĭō, pervĕnīre, pervēnī, perventum: to come to; to reach; to arrive;

pōrtus, -ūs, m.: port, harbor;

prāesĭdĭŭm, -ī, n.: guard; garrison, detachment;

prōvīncĭa, -ae, f.: province; office; duty; command;

-que, conjunction: “and”

rĕcĭpĭō, recipere, recēpī, receptum: to keep back; to take back;

rĕdĕō, redīre, rediī (also redīvī), reditum: to return, go back, give back;

sē (also appears as sēsē), suī, pronoun: himself, herself, itself, themselves

tē, pronoun, accusative masculine or feminine singular of tū "you"

trāns, preposition + acc.: across, over; beyond;

trānsĕō, trānsīre, trānsiī, trānsitum: to go over, cross;

tū, tuī, sing. pron.: you (sing.); yourself

ūrbs, urbis, f.: city; City of Rome;

ūsquĕ, adv. up to, towards

vĕnĭō, venīre, vēnī, ventum: to come;

vōs, vestrum, pl. pron.: you (pl.)
