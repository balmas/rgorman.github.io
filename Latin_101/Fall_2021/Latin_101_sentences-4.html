Translation Sentences 4

1
mīlitibus signum dedit.
mīlitibus is DAT. What is its grammatical function?
2
equitātuī omnī praefuit.
Praesum takes a dative complement. Refer to the verb frame in the vocab.
3
pecūniam Diōnī dedit.
What is the verb frame of dedit (dō)? How does a DAT fit in?
4
attulit auxilium reliquīs.
What is the verb frame of attulit (adferō). How does at DAT fit in?
5
ille omnibus praesidiō fuit.
omnibus is DAT of beneficiary; praesidiō is DAT of purpose.
6
ille exercituī posteā praefuit.
See verb frame of praefuit in vocab.
7
epistula tua grātissima fuit mihi.
grātissima is a predicate nominal (PNOM)
8
lēgātīs senātus ea mandāta dedit.
What do we call a DAT like lēgātīs?
9
grātae mihi tuae litterae fuērunt.
grātae is a PNOM, mihi a beneficiary.
10
Calēnus litterās accipit ā Caesare.
Nothing too tricky here.
11
Caesar legiōnem nōnum subsidiō dūcit.
subsidiō is a DAT of purpose: "for a help;" that is "to be of assistance."
12
prōvinciae Quīntum Calēnum praeficit.
Refer to the verb frame of praeficit in the vocab.
13
eundem Achillam omnibus cōpiīs praeficit.
Refer to the verb frame of praeficit in the vocab.
14
senātus ei reī Mārcum Scaurum praefēcit.
reī is a DAT complement as per the verb frame of praefēcit.
15
id mīlitibus fuit pergrātum et iūcundum.
pergrātum is a PNOM and mīlitibus is a beneficiary. What is iūcundum?
16
quīnque cohortīs castrīs praesidiō relinquit.
quīnque is FEM PLUR ACC; note that most Latin cardinal numbers have the same form for all genders and cases. The exceptions are "one" (ūnus, -a, -um) "two" (duo, duae, duo), and "three" (trēs, tria).
17
eōdem diē Funisulānus ā tē attulit litterās.
diē is ABL of time.
18
prōvinciae Galliae Mārcus Frontēius praefuit.
To properly understand the syntax of prōvinciae you most know the verb frame of praefuit. Explain.
19
ei mūnītiōnī Titum Labiēnum lēgātum praefēcit.
To properly understand the syntax of mūnītiōnī you most know the verb frame of praefēcit. Explain.
20
Pompeius quīnque legiōnēs subsidiō suīs dūxit.
subsidiō is DAT of purpose, suīs is DAT of beneficiary.
21
pecūniam per Volcātium accipit ab duōbus frātribus.
What do the two prep. phrases mean?
22
Caesar Labiēnum cum cohortibus sex subsidiō mittit.
The cardinal number sex has the same form in all genders and cases.
23
ei negōtiō Quīntum Fūfium lēgātum praeficit.
Refer to the verb frame of praeficio in the vocab. Note that ei is an attribute of negōtiō.
