Translation Sentences 9

1
Carthāginem īre iussī sunt.
Carthāginem: ACC name of a city = limit of motion ("to"). īre = verb of Infinitive Complement Clause (ICC).
2
mē Pompeius Capuam īre voluit.
Capuam: ACC name of city. īre = verb of ICC; mē: ACC = SBJ of ICC.
3
tē tamen sollicitum esse nōlō.
esse = verb of ICC; tē: ACC = SBJ of ICC.
4
volō et esse et habērī grātus.
esse & habērī are verbs of ICCs, with no expressed SBJ. grātus agrees in gender, number, and case with the assumed SBJ. Note that habeō can mean "hold" in the sense of "believe."
5
statuērunt bellī facere fīnem.
facere = verb of ICC; it has no expressed SBJ.
6
et bonī et beātī volumus esse.
esse - verb of ICC with no expressed SBJ. bonī & beātī are both Masc/Plur/Nom, but are not SBJ. What can they be?
7
pugnāre contrā mē īnstituistis.
pugnāre = verb of ICC.
8
Sīlius tē cum Clōdiō loquī vult.
loquī = verb of ICC; it is Deponent. tē: ACC = SBJ of ICC.
9
equitum partem sē sequī iussit.
sequī = verb of ICC; it is Deponent. partem: ACC = SBJ of ICC. equitum = Plur/Gen of pattern 3c.
10
voluit ille senātum interficere.
interficere = verb of ICC; it has no expressed ACC SBJ.
11

venīre = verb of ICC; it has no expressed SBJ.
12

esse = verb of ICC. mē: ACC = SBJ of ICC.
13

14

facere = verb of ICC; it has no expressed ACC SBJ.
15

ferre = verb of ICC; it has no expressed ACC SBJ. The verb ferō can mean "endure."
