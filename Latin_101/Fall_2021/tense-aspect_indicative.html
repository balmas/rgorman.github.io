Tense and Aspect of Latin INdicatives
Past time Present time Future time
Completed aspect
Latin: pluperfect tense
Eng.: -ed(have) + -en(verb-x)
example: "had bitten"
Latin: perfect tense
Eng.: -s(have) + -en(verb-x)
example: "has bitten"
Latin: future perfect tense
Eng.: will + -base(have) + -en(verb-x)
example: "will have bitten"
Simple aspect
Latin: perfect tense
Eng.: -ed(verb-x)
example: "bit"
Latin: present tense
Eng.: -s(verb-x)
example: "bites"
Latin: future tense
Eng.: will + -base(verb-x)
example: "will bite"
Progressive aspect
Latin: imperfect tense
Eng.: -ed(be) + -ing(verb-x)
example: "was biting"
Latin: present tense
Eng.: -s (be) + -ing(verb-x)
example: "is biting"
Latin: future tense
Eng.: will + -base(be) + -ing(verb-x)
example: "will be biting"
