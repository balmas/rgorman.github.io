Translation Sentences 5

1
Caesar impetum eius sustinuit.
eius is M/G/S and it is a substative; translate "of that guy" or "his." sustinuit means "to hold back" in the sense of sucessfully holding off and attack.
2
Marsī eam oppidī partem occupant.
Note the possition of oppidī. A genitive is often sandwiched between a noun and its attributive adjective.
3
ibi castra duārum legiōnum facit.
legiōnum is GEN PLUR of the 3c pattern
4
mē ōrātiō Lūcullī dē ipsā rē mōvit.
The prep. phrase dē ipsā rē here modifies ōrātiō rather than the verb. This often happens.
5
Crassus ad hostium castra contendit.
hostium is GEN PLUR of the 3i pattern. Genitives are often placed between a prep. and its object. contendit here means "hurry"or "hasten." It could be either present or perfect.
6
fuit in Ciliciā mēcum tribūnus mīlitum.
mīlitum is GEN PLUR of 3c pattern. Remember that mēcum = cum mē.
7
ab eō locō in fīnīs Ambiānōrum pervēnit.
in fīnīs ("into the boudaries") means "into the territory."
8
prīmum impetum nostrōrum sustinuērunt.
nostrōrum is MAS/GEN/PLUR and is a substantive adjective. Translate "of our guys." sustinuērunt means "held back" in the sense of sucessfully holding off and attack.
9
Conōn cum parte nāvium in patriam venit.
nāvium is GEN/PLUR of 3i pattern.
10
magnum hostium numerum paucī sustinuērunt.
hostium is GEN/PLUR of 3i pattern. paucī is a substantive adj. MAS/NOM/PLUR.
11
Alcibiadēs ad exercitum vēnit Athēniēnsium.
Athēniēnsium is MAS/GEN/PLUR of pattern 3i. It is a substantive adjective
12
huius classis mīlle nāvium longārum fuit.
huius is MASC/GEN/SING and is a substantive adj. Translate "of that guy" or "his." classis is NOM. mīlle is NOM functioning as PNOM. nāvium is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3i.
13
celeriter ex cōnspectū castrōrum discessit.
Not too difficult.
14
Caesar in fīnīs Sugambrōrum contendit.
in fīnīs ("into the boudaries") means "into the territory." contendit ("hurry") can be either present or perfect.
15
Saburra cōpiās equitum peditumque īnstruit.
equitum and peditum are both GEN/PLUR of pattern 3c.
16
omnēs Eburōnum et Nerviōrum cōpiae discēdunt.
Eburōnum is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3c.
17
Magnōs inpetūs legiōnum equitātūsque sustinet.
inpetūs is ACC/PLUR of pattern 4; legiōnum is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3c; equitātūs is GEN/SING of pattern 4.
18
ex magnā parte Morinōrum ad eum lēgātī vēnērunt.
lēgātī is NOM/PLUR.
19
duās ibi legiōnēs relīquit et partem auxiliōrum.
Not to complicated.
20
ab Ōcelō in fīnīs Vocontiōrum diē septimō pervēnit.
diē septimō is ABL of time.
21
interim ā complūribus cīvitātibus ad eum lēgātī veniunt.
No genitive in this sentence!
22
Fabius magnīs itineribus ad exercitum Afrānī contendit.
magnīs itineribus is a military technical term meaning "forced marches." This means marches at a pace greater than usual and without the usual rest breaks. Afrānī is GEN/SING of pattern 2.
23
Caesar per hostium mūnītiōnēs in suam partem oppidī venit.
hostium is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3i.
24
Teutomatus cum magnō equitum suōrum numerō ad eum pervēnit.
equitum is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3c.
25
Caesar castra ad flūmen Apsum pōnit in fīnibus Apolloniātium.
Apolloniātium is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3i.
26
complūrēs tribūnī mīlitum et centuriōnēs ad Caesarem veniunt.
mīlitum is GEN/PLUR of pattern 3c.
27
castra movet diēbusque circiter quīndecim ad fīnīs Belgārum pervēnit.
The adjective quīndecim, like other cardinal numbers above trēs, has the same form in all genders and cases. Here quīndecim is ABL and therefore is an attribute of diēbus. Belgārum is GEN/PLUR of pattern 1.
28
post diem quārtum cum longīs paucīs nāvibus in cōnspectum Āfricae venit.
The phrase longīs nāvibus is a military technical term meaning "warships." Āfricae is GEN/SING of pattern 1.
29
altera castra ad alteram oppidī partem pōnit; hīs castrīs Cūriōnem praefēcit.
oppidī is GEN/SING of pattern 2.
30
ipse dē quārtā vigiliā eōdem itinere ad eōs contendit equitātumque omnem ante sē mittit.
dē quārtā vigiliā is technical military language, meaning "after the fourth watch." Roman military practice divided the night into 4 segment, each of which was called a vigilia ("watch"). The preposition dē does not normally mean "after" as it does in this phrase.
31
Caesar posterō diē ex omnibus praesidiīs cohortīs dēdūxit atque omnis suās cōpiās in campō īnstrūxit.
No genitive here! praesidiīs is ABL but cohortīs is ACC.
