Translation Sentences 8:
Present, Imperfect, Perfect and Pluperfect Active and Passive
All persons and numbers.

1
mittō ad tē Trebātium.
mittō is 1st person sing. present active.
2
pācis enim auctor erās.
erās is 2nd person sing. imperfect.
3
Rōmae posteā nōn fuistī.
fuistī is 2nd person sing. perfect. Rōmae is SING LOCATIVE.
4
Tulliam posteā mēcum teneō.
teneō is 1st person sing. present active.
5
mānsī eō diē in Sinuessānō.
mānsī is 1st person sing. perfect active.
6
adhūc in hāc sum sententiā.
sum is 1st person sing. present.
7
et audiō tē et videō libenter.
audiō & videō are 1st person sing present active. et … et can be translated as "both … and"
8
tum enim erāmus in maximā spē.
erāmus is 1st person plur. imperfect.
9
eōdem diē dās alterās litterās.
dās is 2nd person sing. present active.
10
tenuistī prōvinciam per annōs decem.
tenuistī is 2nd person sing. perfect active.
11
sed nōn loquor dē nōbīs, dē illīs loquor.
loquor is 1st person sing. present DEPONENT.
12
ego ex ipsā rē magnam capiō voluptātem.
capiō is 1st person sing. present active.
13
ego ad Caesarem ūnās Capuā litterās dedī.
dedī is 1st person sing. perfect active. It is a verb often used for sending a letter. Capuā is FEM/SING/ABL. What can ABL mean with the name of a city/small island?
14
tū enim ad mē scrīpsistī Coccēī respōnsum.
scrīpsistī is 2nd person sing. perfect active.
15
anteā capiēbam ex officiō meō voluptātem.
capiēbam is 1st person sing. imperfect active.
16
exercitum Apollōniā Dyrrachiōque mōvistī.
mōvistī is 2nd person sing. perfect active. Apollōniā & Dyrrachiō are ABL city names.
17
eā enim ipsā hōrā accēperam tuās litterās.
accēperam is 1st person sing. pluperfect active.
18
ipse nihil scrībō, legō autem libentissimē.
scrībō & legō are 1st person sing. present active.
19
nōn ego dē meīs, sed dē vestrīs factīs loquor.
loquor is 1st sing. pres. DEPONENT.
20
spem omnem in Decimō Brūtō et Plancō habēmus.
habēmus is 1st plur. pres. act.
21
Libōnem mēcum habeō et habueram anteā Cascam.
habeō: 1st sing present active; habueram: 1st sing pluperf act.
22
Quīntum frātrem hībernīs et Ciliciae praefēcī.
praefēcī: 1st sing perfect act. hībernīs & Ciliciae are DAT; see verb frame in vocab.
23
sed in hōc homine nūllam accipiō excūsātiōnem.
accipiō: 1st sing present act.
24
ē Thessaliā Brundisium cum legiōnibus revertistī.
revertistī: 2nd sing perfect active (an odd form with a deponent present system and a regular perfect system). Brundisium is ACC proper name of a city.
25
id ego quoque audieram et herī ad tē scrīpseram.
audieram & scrīpseram are 1st sing pluperf act.
26
sed veniō iam ad Lūcium Cassium, familiārem meum.
veniō: 1st sing present active. Lūcium Cassium irefers to a single person. We will discuss apposition.
27
laudābam incrēdibilem dīligentiam Gnaeī Pompeī.
laudābam: 1st sing imperfect active. Gnaeī Pompeī is the name of an individual.
28
haec ego ex Pūbliō Vēdiō, Pompeī familiārī, audīvī.
audīvī: 1st sing perfect active. Pūbliō Vēdiō is one name.
29
ad Dolābellam Tīrōnem mīsī cum mandātīs et litterās.
mīsī: 1st sing perfect act. Dolābellam & Tīrōnem refer to different people.
30
liber tuus et lēctus est et legitur ā mē dīligenter.
lēctus est: 3rd sing perfect passive; legitur 3rd sing present passive.
31
tēcum Ephesī dē rē Mārcī Anneī, lēgātī meī, locūtus sum.
locūtus sum 1st sing perfect deponent. Ephesī is LOCATIVE. Mārcī Anneī is a single name.
32
ego Gāium Pomptīnum, lēgātum meum, Brundisī exspectābam.
exspectābam: 1st sing imperfect active. Gāium Pomptīnum is a single name. Brundisī is LOCATIVE.
33
cēterōs in prōvinciam eius modī hominēs cum imperiō mittimus.
mittimus: 1st plural present active.
34
nōs Brundisī apud Mārcum Laēnium Flaccum diēs tredecim fuimus.
fuimus: 1st plural perfect active. Brundisī is LOC. Mārcum Laēnium Flaccum is a single name. diēs tredecim is ACC of extent of time.
35
ego autem et tuās litterās lēgī libenter et audīvī Lūcīlium dīligenter.
lēgī & audīvī: 1st sing perfect active.
36
Lentulus suō in nōs officiō spem nōn nūllam affert Pompēī voluntātis.
affert: 3rd sing present act.
37
Brūtō tuae litterae grātae erant. fuī enim apud illum multās hōrās in Nēside.
erant: 3rd plural imperfect active. fuī 1st sing perfect active. multās hōrās: ACC of extent of time.
38
Phameae causam recēperam ipsīus quidem causā; erat enim mihi sānē familiāris.
recēperam: 1st sing pluperfect active. erat: 3rd sing imperfect active. The ABL causā usually means "for the sake of" and is completed by a GEN (here ipsīus).
39
itaque in Cappadociā extrēmā nōn longa ā Taurō apud oppidum Cybistra castra fēcī.
fēcī: 1st sing perfect active.
40
dē mandātīs Caesaris adventūque Labiēnī et respōnsīs cōnsulum ac Pompēī scrīpsī ad tē.
scrīpsī: 1st sing perfect active.
