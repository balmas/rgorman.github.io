Latin 350
Joseph and his Brothers, part 1

1
Habitavit autem Jacob in terra Chanaan, in qua pater suus peregrinatus est.
  • Jacob: Hebrew proper names are often left uninflected in the Vulgate; they have only the one form in all cases. The Hebrew Yaʻăqôb means "heel-catcher."
  • Chanaan: a place name (Hbr. Kᵉnaʻan) meaning "lowland." It refers to certain land on the west side of the Jordan River.
2
Et hae sunt generationes ejus:
  • This sentence brings the concluding section to a conclusion; it is not the introduction to this section, which is not about the descendents of Jacob.
3
Joseph cum sedecim esset annorum, pascebat gregem cum fratribus suis adhuc puer:
  • Joseph: a PN (Hbr. Yôwçêph, "[God] has added").
  • sedecim ... annorum: While nominative case is most usual, it is not uncommon to find nouns in the genitive functioning as Predicate Nominals (PNom) dependent on a form of sum.
  • adhuc puer: a secondary predication. We can translate "while still a boy" or the like.
4
et erat cum filiis Balae et Zelphae uxorum patris sui:
  • Balae et Zelphae uxorum: Jacob had children with several women. Here are mentioned Bilhah (Hebr. Bilhâh, "timid/modest one") and Zilpah (Hebr. Zilpâh, "a trickling"). These women were, respectively, the servants of Rachel and Leah, Jacob's wives.
5
accusavitque fratres suos apud patrem crimine pessimo.
  • accusavit: the frame of asscuso here is: [SBJ accuses X of Y] where X is ACC and Y is ABL.
  • crimine pessimo: the details of Joseph's accusation are not specified.
6
Israël autem diligebat Joseph super omnes filios suos, eo quod in senectute genuisset eum:
  • Israël: Hebr. Yisrâʼêl, "God prevails. A second name for Jacob.
  • eo quod: when quod appears in its function as causal subordinator (as here), the matrix clause "can also contain a corresponding causal expression" (OLS II. 283). Here that corresponding expression is eo, "from that [fact]."
7
fecitque ei tunicam polymitam.
  • polymitam: "with many threads," often interpreted as "with many colors of threads."
8
Videntes autem fratres ejus quod a patre plus cunctis filiis amaretur, oderant eum, ne -c poterant ei quidquam pacifice loqui.
  • Videntes: a circumstantial participle. Compare the difference between "the boy swimming near the reef was eaten by the shark," and "swimming near the reef, the boy was eaten by the shark."
  • quod: beginning with Teutullian (d. c. 220 CE) Christian writers increasingly use quod + finite verb for indirect statement clauses. Earlier writers preferred ACC + infinive for this construction. quod functions as a complementizer and can be translated "that."
  • cunctis filiis: ABL of comparison; translate "than all the[other] children/sons."
  • amaretur: the Subjunctive mood probably indicates that the content of the clause is not vouched for by the author.
  • oderant: this verb has no present stem, and therefore forms built with the present stem take on "present-stem meanings." For example, the pluperfect has the meaning usually carried by the perfect (simple past or completed present).
9
Accidit quoque ut visum somnium referret fratribus suis:
  • Accidit: the relevant frame is [SBJ happened] where SBJ is an ut + Subjunctive clause. We can often translate "it happened that ..." where "it" is not a true subject, but an expletive (place-holder).
  • visum somnium: in Latin, dreams are "seen" while in English we "have" dreams. Thus we might translate visum somnium, "seen dream" as "a dream he had" or the like.
10
quae causa majoris odii seminarium fuit.
  • quae: Latin is confortable with relative words that refer to previous sentences; English does not like it. Translate quae as "this."
11
Dixitque ad eos: Audite somnium meum quod vidi:
  • Audite ... vidi: It is often unclear whether it is better to treat a direct quotation as a dependency of another clause or as a separate sentence.
12
putabam nos ligare manipulos in agro: et quasi consurgere manipulum meum, et stare, vestrosque manipulos circumstantes adorare manipulum meum.
  • manipulos: here "bundles" of harvested stalks of grain, "sheaves."
